Our COURSES

ANM [Auxiliary Nursing Midwifery]

Auxiliary Nursing Midwifery or ANM for short, is a 2 year undergraduate diploma course in nursing. The minimum eligibility criteria to get admitted into Auxiliary Nursing Midwifery course is to be a minimum 10+2 level pass out with a minimum aggregate score of at least 40%. ANM students are educated on maternity care and providing assistance to new mothers during pregnancy and post-delivery.

GNM [General Nursing and Midwifery]

It is a 3½ years diploma in paramedics. Nursing and Midwifery focuses on the provision of care to individuals, families, and communities. The course can be pursued after successful completion of the 10+2 level of education in the any Stream stream from a recognized educational board with a minimum aggregate score of 40%.
